# Whole-Site Archive Content Design

## Goal
Supplement the full site with real project materials from `Phase2_IniBuilding/Resources/Ori/Elements` while preserving the existing route structure and maintaining a restrained, premium visual tone.

## Confirmed Constraints
- Use the current multi-page site architecture.
- Bring in materials from `Elements`, including:
  - photos
  - publication pages
  - infographics
  - brand graphics
- New text must stay descriptive and image-serving only.
- Do not add new narrative copy, long explanations, or promotional storytelling.
- Keep the visual tone restrained, quiet, and premium.

## Content Strategy
- Treat the site as an editorial archive rather than a campaign landing page.
- Let images, documents, and diagrams carry most of the information density.
- Use short captions for:
  - time
  - place
  - event
  - material type
  - depicted subject
- Preserve strong whitespace and avoid turning pages into dense PPT-like boards.

## Approved Direction
- Overall mode: archive exhibition with selective editorial pacing.
- Home keeps the lightest media density.
- About, Journey, and Field Research hold the strongest archive density.
- Actions and Voices become quieter image-led pages.
- Join Us stays functional and restrained, using evidence materials rather than long invitations.

## Visual Rules
- One page should have at most one true hero-scale image.
- Document pages and infographics must sit in flatter archive frames with generous whitespace.
- Photo groups should use stable aspect ratios and consistent borders.
- Keep hover behavior subtle and existing-site-compatible.
- Avoid turning any page into a dense collage or a slideshow aesthetic.

## Caption Rules
- Each asset gets:
  - title
  - optional meta line
  - one short descriptive sentence at most
- Captions may describe:
  - event
  - location
  - date or period
  - depicted object or action
  - document type
- Captions may not introduce new narrative claims or long explanatory copy.

## Architecture Rules
- Keep the current route structure and page files.
- Centralize archive content in a shared content module.
- Use a small set of reusable archive display components so spacing, framing, and captions stay consistent.
- Keep homepage media density lighter than interior pages.

## Validation
- No page should retain `TBD` placeholders.
- No page should introduce non-descriptive long-form copy.
- Imported materials must feel curated, not dumped.
- Desktop and mobile should both preserve clarity and whitespace.
- Build must pass with all local media references resolved.
